The Siege of Jerusalem Electronic Archive is an interactive platform for the study of manuscript copies of the 14th-century poem The Siege of Jerusalem that features transcriptions, descriptions, and images of all known manuscript versions of the poem. The SJEA is edited and maintained by Timothy L. Stinson. Manuscripts were transcribed by Stinson and encoded in XML by Stinson and John Ivor Carlson. Manuscript descriptions were written by Stinson. Transcriptions and descriptions were encoded using the Text Encoding Initiative’s P5 guidelines. Rights to all images of manuscripts are retained by the libraries owning those manuscripts; use of the images is subject to the guidelines stated under Terms and Conditions.


The miniature depicted on the Home page is from British Library, MS Egerton 2781 fol. 190r. Egerton 2781, also known as the Neville of Hornby Hours, is an English manuscript dating to the second quarter of the 14th century, roughly half a century before the composition of The Siege of Jerusalem. The miniature depicts Titus and Vespasian’s siege of Jerusalem’s walls. The image is used here courtesy of The British Library, and may not be duplicated or used without the library’s permission.
